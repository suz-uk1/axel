Access control

Q: Is MFA required?
A: MFA is required for administrative access. Access is granted by role and reviewed periodically (least privilege).

Q: How do you provision/deprovision access?
A: Access is provisioned through a documented process based on role. Access is removed promptly upon role change or termination. Administrative actions are logged.

Encryption

Q: Is data encrypted in transit?
A: Yes. We use TLS for data in transit.

Q: Is data encrypted at rest?
A: Yes. Encryption at rest is enabled where supported by our storage providers and configured for relevant data stores.

Logging & monitoring

Q: Do you log security events?
A: Yes. We log key security events (authentication, administrative actions, and system events) and monitor for anomalies. Logs are retained for an appropriate period to support investigations.

Incident response

Q: Do you have an incident response plan?
A: Yes. We maintain an incident response process for triage, containment, eradication, recovery, and post-incident review.

Q: What is your breach notification timeframe?
A: We notify customers without undue delay in line with contractual and legal requirements, providing details as information becomes available.

BCDR

Q: Do you have backups?
A: Yes. Backups are performed and we periodically test restoration.

Q: What are your RTO/RPO?
A: We document recovery objectives appropriate to the service. (If you have numbers, state them; otherwise state your current objective and roadmap.)

Vendors / subprocessors

Q: Do you use subprocessors?
A: Yes, for infrastructure and operational tooling. We assess vendors based on risk and require appropriate security controls in contracts.
