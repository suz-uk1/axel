ISO 27001 Evidence Tracker (Lean Startup Guide)

If you’re a small team, the tracker is the difference between “we do the control” and “we can prove it in an audit”. This page explains what to track and how to keep it audit-ready.

Download sample tracker (CSV) Download all samples (ZIP)

What an evidence tracker is

  • A single sheet that maps controls → evidence → owner → cadence.
  • It turns ISO 27001 from “docs” into a repeatable operating rhythm.

The minimum columns (recommended)

  • Control (Annex A ref or your internal control ID)
  • Evidence item (what proves it)
  • Where stored (link/folder path)
  • Owner (person accountable)
  • Frequency (monthly/quarterly/annual)
  • Last collected / Next due
  • Notes (exceptions, screenshots needed, etc.)

How to keep it audit-ready (startup version)

  • Make “Next due” a weekly review item (15 minutes).
  • Use a shared evidence folder with a fixed structure (by control or by process).
  • Prefer screenshots/exports with timestamps (e.g., MFA settings, access reviews, backup restore test proof).

Common failure modes

  • Tracker exists but evidence links are stale.
  • Owners are missing (so nothing is maintained).
  • Frequency is unclear (so evidence is collected too late).
