1) Scope & context

  • Scope statement exists and matches reality (systems/teams/locations).
  • Out-of-scope items are explicitly listed.

2) Risk method & risk register

  • Methodology defines likelihood/impact and acceptance criteria.
  • Risk register uses that methodology consistently (no arbitrary scoring).

3) SoA and control decisions

  • SoA exists; each control has applicability + justification.
  • Controls marked “implemented” have evidence references.

4) RTP and execution

  • Non-acceptable risks have treatments with owner + due date.
  • Open actions are tracked (tickets/backlog) and mapped to RTP entries.

5) Operational evidence

  • Access reviews exist (at least one cycle).
  • Incident log exists (even if empty, show the log + procedure).
  • Change approvals are traceable (PR reviews / change tickets).
  • Supplier assessments exist for critical vendors.

6) Management system

  • Internal audit report + findings log exist.
  • Corrective actions (CAPA) are owned and closed-loop.
  • Management review minutes exist with decisions/actions.
