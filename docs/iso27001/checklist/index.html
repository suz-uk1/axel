ISO 27001 “Consistency” 1‑Page Checklist (Startup Edition)

Goal: remove audit rework by keeping one clear line from Scope → Risk → SoA → Evidence → Audit → Mgmt Review.

0) Inputs (have these ready)

  • Your product/service diagram (what you sell + how it runs)
  • Current asset inventory (even rough)
  • List of 3rd parties (cloud, SaaS, MSP, contractors)
  • Incident / backup / access control reality (what you *actually* do today)

1) Scope (make it explicit)

Write 3 sentences:

1. *What* is in scope (service/system, locations, people)

2. *What* is out of scope (and why)

3. Scope boundary (networks, repos, cloud accounts, environments)

Checks:

  • ☐ Scope matches reality (not wishful)
  • ☐ Interfaces to out‑of‑scope systems are listed
  • ☐ Critical suppliers in scope are named (or justified)

2) Risk assessment (connect to the scope)

Minimum viable method:

  • Assets → Threats → Vulnerabilities → Impacts → Existing controls → Residual risk

Checks:

  • ☐ Every top asset is in-scope
  • ☐ You have at least 1 risk per: identity, data, availability, suppliers
  • ☐ Treatment decision recorded (accept / reduce / avoid / transfer)

3) Statement of Applicability (SoA) — your “truth table”

For each control (Annex A / your control set):

  • Applicable? (Y/N)
  • Justification
  • Implementation status (planned / partial / implemented)
  • Evidence pointer (link or doc ID)

Checks:

  • ☐ Every “implemented” control has evidence
  • ☐ Every “not applicable” has defensible justification
  • ☐ The SoA references the risk treatment plan

4) Evidence (the part that wins audits)

Rule: one control → one evidence artifact (minimum).

Examples:

  • Access control → RBAC policy + sample access review record
  • Backups → backup policy + restore test record
  • Incident mgmt → incident runbook + tabletop exercise record
  • Supplier mgmt → vendor questionnaire + onboarding review record

Checks:

  • ☐ Evidence is dated and repeatable (not one‑off)
  • ☐ Links work and are accessible to auditors
  • ☐ Evidence matches what the policy says

5) Internal audit → corrective actions

Checks:

  • ☐ Audit plan exists (what/when/who)
  • ☐ Findings include root cause + action owner + due date
  • ☐ Follow-up verifies effectiveness

6) Management review (close the loop)

Agenda checklist:

  • Performance vs objectives
  • Major risks + changes (org/product/suppliers)
  • Nonconformities + corrective action status
  • Resource needs

Checks:

  • ☐ Decisions and actions recorded
  • ☐ Actions feed back into risk/SoA

Quick self‑test (if you answer “no”, expect rework)

  • Can you trace **one risk** → **control** → **evidence** in under 60 seconds?
  • Can you explain why each “N/A” control is truly out of scope?
  • Do your policies describe what you actually do day‑to‑day?
