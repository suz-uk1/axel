ISO 27001:2022 — Minimum Viable ISMS (Startup Checklist)

This is a practical “start here” checklist to get from zero → credible Stage 1 readiness without document sprawl.

Not legal advice. No certification guarantee. Use judgement and align with your auditor/certification body expectations.

0) Define scope (do this first)

Deliverable: ISMS Scope Statement - What products/services, locations, and teams are in scope? - What’s explicitly out of scope (for now)?

Evidence to prepare - High-level architecture diagram - Data flow / system boundaries - Org chart or responsibility map

1) Risk method (keep it simple)

Deliverable: Risk Assessment Methodology - Asset-based or scenario-based (pick one) - Likelihood/impact scales (e.g., 1–5) - Risk acceptance criteria (what counts as “acceptable”)

Evidence to prepare - Defined scoring rubric - A few example scored risks

2) Perform a risk assessment

Deliverable: Risk Register - Key information assets - Threats/vulnerabilities - Inherent risk → treatment decision

Evidence to prepare - Asset inventory (even if lightweight) - Links to relevant tickets/docs showing risk decisions

3) Decide controls (SoA)

Deliverable: Statement of Applicability (SoA) - Which Annex A controls apply and why - Implementation status + references to evidence

Evidence to prepare - Control-to-evidence links (policies, logs, tickets)

4) Plan treatments

Deliverable: Risk Treatment Plan (RTP) - For each non-acceptable risk: mitigations, owner, due date, status

Evidence to prepare - Backlog items (Jira/Linear/GitHub issues) mapped to RTP

5) Core policies & procedures (minimum set)

You can keep these short, but they must be real.

Typical minimum - Information Security Policy - Access control (incl. joiner/mover/leaver) - Incident management - Change management - Supplier/third-party security - Asset management (how you track key assets)

Evidence to prepare - Access reviews (screenshots/log exports) - On/Offboarding checklists - Incident log (even if “no incidents”, show the log) - Change tickets / PR approvals - Vendor assessment notes for critical vendors

6) Run an internal audit

Deliverable: Internal Audit Plan + Audit Report + Findings log - Audit your ISMS against your own policies and ISO requirements

Evidence to prepare - Audit checklist - Findings with corrective actions assigned

7) Management review

Deliverable: Management Review Agenda + Minutes - ISMS performance, audit findings, incidents, risks, improvements

Evidence to prepare - Minutes with decisions/actions - KPI snapshot (e.g., security training completion, incidents, vuln SLAs)

8) Corrective actions (CAPA)

Deliverable: Corrective Action Log - Root cause, action, owner, due date, verification

Evidence to prepare - Closed-loop proof (before/after, ticket links)

A lightweight 4–8 week plan (example)

  • Week 1: scope + risk method
  • Week 2: risk register v1 + asset inventory
  • Week 3: SoA v1 + RTP v1
  • Week 4: core policies/procedures finalized + start evidence collection
  • Week 5: supplier risk + access reviews + incident/change evidence
  • Week 6: internal audit + CAPA
  • Week 7: management review
  • Week 8: Stage 1 scheduling + tidy evidence package
