Minimum required elements

  • Risk identification approach (asset-based or scenario-based)
  • Likelihood scale + definitions
  • Impact scale + definitions
  • Risk calculation (e.g., L×I)
  • Risk acceptance criteria (what is acceptable / needs treatment)
  • Review cadence and roles

Example scoring (1–5)

Likelihood (L)
1 Rare
2 Unlikely
3 Possible
4 Likely
5 Almost certain

Impact (I)
1 Negligible
2 Minor
3 Moderate
4 Major
5 Severe

Risk score = L × I (1–25)

Acceptance criteria (example)

  • 1–6: Accept (document justification)
  • 7–12: Treat if low effort; otherwise accept with compensating controls
  • 13–25: Must treat (RTP with owner + due date)

Auditor-friendly tips

  • Define terms (what is “Major” impact?) so scoring is repeatable.
  • Show at least a few scored examples and link to treatments in your RTP.
  • Keep scope aligned: don’t score systems that are out of ISMS scope.
