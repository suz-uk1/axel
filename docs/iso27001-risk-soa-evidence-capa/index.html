ISO 27001 consistency check:
Risk → SoA → Evidence → CAPA

If you’re a startup building ISO 27001, most wasted time (and many audit findings) come from one thing: your documents drift out of sync.

Get the Startup Toolkit Jump to checklist

What “drift” looks like

  • Your Risk Register says a risk matters, but your SoA excludes the related control (or has weak rationale).
  • Your SoA includes a control, but you can’t quickly point to evidence (owner, system, artifact, frequency).
  • You have findings from internal audit, but there’s no clear CAPA closure (action, owner, due date, verification).

Quick checklist (10 minutes)

  1. Pick 5 top risks (by likelihood×impact). For each, list the Annex A controls you’re using to treat it.
  2. Open your SoA: verify each control has a real rationale tied to those risks (not generic text).
  3. For 10 controls: record evidence in one table: Control → Evidence → Owner → System → Frequency.
  4. For 3 recent issues: ensure they have CAPA entries with due dates and verification notes.

If you can’t do these steps quickly, you’re not “behind on ISO” — you’re missing an evidence workflow.
