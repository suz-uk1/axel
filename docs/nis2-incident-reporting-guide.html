NIS2 incident reporting (24h / 72h): a practical setup guide

Most teams don’t fail NIS2 on tooling. They fail on timestamps, accountability, and evidence. This guide helps you set up a defensible workflow in under 30 minutes.

1) Define T0 (the most common confusion)

  • T0 = the moment your organisation becomes aware of a significant incident.
  • Record Detected_At and Known_At_T0 separately in your incident log.

2) Track the deadlines as fields, not as memory

  • Create fields for T0+24h and T0+72h due times.
  • Assign an Incident Commander and a Triage Owner for every SEV1/SEV2.

3) Keep evidence by default

  • Link the ticket, key logs, comms drafts, and decisions in an Evidence_Links field.
  • If you can’t show it, it didn’t happen (from an auditor’s perspective).

4) Run one tabletop

  • Pick a realistic scenario (phishing, ransomware, SaaS compromise).
  • Fill one row end-to-end in the timeline tracker.
