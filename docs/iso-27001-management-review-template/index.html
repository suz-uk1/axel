What auditors look for

  • Evidence that top management reviewed ISMS performance
  • Decisions on changes, resources, and improvements
  • Actions with owners + due dates (not vague notes)

Agenda (startup-friendly)

  1. Scope/context changes (products, systems, locations)
  2. Risk posture: top risks, trend, residual risk acceptance
  3. Security objectives/KPIs (what improved/worsened)
  4. Internal audit results + corrective actions (CAPA)
  5. Incidents and nonconformities
  6. Supplier/third-party risks (critical vendors)
  7. Opportunities for improvement
  8. Decisions: actions, owners, deadlines, resourcing
