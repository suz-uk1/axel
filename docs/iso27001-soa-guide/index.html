ISO 27001 Statement of Applicability (SoA)

The SoA is not “just a list of controls.” It’s the index that ties your scope, risks, and control decisions into something an auditor can follow.

What the SoA is (in plain English)

Your SoA should answer, for each Annex A control (27001:2022):

  • Is it applicable to your scope?
  • If yes: what’s the justification and implementation status?
  • If no: what’s the justification for exclusion?
  • Where is the evidence (policy/procedure/log) that supports it?

What auditors look for

  • Traceability: scope → risks → chosen controls → evidence
  • Consistency: your risk treatment plan matches your SoA decisions
  • Justifications: exclusions are defensible (not hand-wavy)
  • Evidence pointers: not “we do this,” but “here’s where it’s proven”

Common SoA mistakes (and how to avoid them)

  • Copy/pasting a generic SoA without linking to your risks → add a “risk reference” column.
  • No evidence mapping → add an “evidence link/location” column.
  • Excluding controls without justification → write one specific sentence: why it’s out of scope.
  • SoA doesn’t match the risk register → treat it as a single system, not separate docs.

Next step: make it startup-sized and consistent
