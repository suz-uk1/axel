ISO 27001 TRACE Checklist

Risk → Control → Evidence. If you can’t do this trace in <60 seconds for your top 5 risks, Stage 1/2 will feel like archaeology.

Use this as a lightweight operating checklist, not a paperwork exercise.

0Setup

  • Pick one scope statement (systems + locations + teams in-scope).
  • Pick one vocabulary for controls (ISO/Annex A) and stick to it.
  • Create a single shared folder/space for evidence links (not screenshots in Slack).

1Top 5 risks

  • Write 5 real risks (not generic) tied to in-scope assets.
  • Each risk has: asset, threat, impact, owner, treatment.

2TRACE table (do this 5 times)

Make one row per risk/control pair. Minimal columns:

  • Risk (ID + 1-liner)
  • Control (SoA control + why it applies)
  • Owner (a person)
  • Cadence (how often the control runs)
  • Evidence link (ticket/log/report/runbook + last run date)

3Evidence quality check

  • Evidence shows the control operates (timestamped), not just exists.
  • Evidence is reproducible: someone else can follow the runbook and re-run it.
  • Evidence points to one source of truth (not a dozen disconnected artifacts).

4Mini internal audit

  • Sample 5–10 controls and verify the trace row matches reality.
  • Log 1–3 findings and open CAPA items with owners + due dates.

5Management review (30 minutes)

  • Review: top risks, open CAPAs, incidents, supplier risk, upcoming changes.
  • Record decisions + actions in meeting minutes (this is audit evidence too).

FAQ

Is this enough to pass? No—passing depends on your practices and auditor sampling. This checklist prevents the most common “traceability” failures.

Where does SoA fit? The SoA is the “control claim.” The evidence link proves the claim is operational.
