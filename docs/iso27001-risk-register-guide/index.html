ISO 27001 Risk Register

A risk register is the engine of your ISMS: it explains what can go wrong, how bad it is, and what you’re doing about it—in a way that’s traceable to controls and evidence.

What auditors expect

  • Clear scope/context (what assets/processes are in-scope)
  • Consistent scoring (likelihood × impact)
  • Defined risk owners + due dates
  • Treatment decisions that link to controls
  • Evidence that treatment is actually implemented

Minimum columns (startup-sized)

  • Risk ID
  • Asset / process (what is impacted)
  • Threat + vulnerability
  • Impact + Likelihood
  • Inherent risk (before controls)
  • Treatment (avoid/mitigate/transfer/accept)
  • Controls (Annex A refs) + owner
  • Residual risk (after controls) + acceptance
  • Evidence link (policy/log/ticket)

Common mistakes

  • Only listing threats without the vulnerable condition → auditors can’t see why it’s real.
  • No owners/dates → it becomes a static spreadsheet, not a managed system.
  • Residual risk never changes → implies controls aren’t implemented or scoring is meaningless.
  • No links to SoA → your SoA becomes an ungrounded checklist.

How it connects to your SoA

The simplest mental model:

  • Risk register says what you’re treating.
  • SoA says which controls you chose (and why).
  • Evidence map says how you prove it.

When audits go badly, it’s usually because these three drift.
